The Paper

My paper was an analysis about "Universal Basic Income." I already turned it in and I feel confident in both my analysis and citations. I have never writen this much before.

Intro Paragraph:

The Western World, following the COVID-19 pandemic, has experienced a socioeconomic paradigm shift, with the alienating lockdown policies causing the world to reflect on both themselves and society as a whole. We can more critically view our politicians, our economy, and our social values through the lenses of our neighbors and our former adversaries, regardless of class. This paradigm shift has perpetuated “radical” and unorthodox policies and ideas to take a foothold in Western capitalist nations. For example, social democratic and socialist platforms are rising in popularity in the United States, even though the demonization and social “book-burning” was McCarthyism. One of these proposed ideas is that of Universal Basic Income (UBI.) Though a concept that has existed for decades, originating from centuries of socio economic advancements, Andrew Yang, the former presidential candidate, had proposed this policy as a compromise between capitalism and socialism. Yang and UBI is reflective of both the historical class antagonism, the current Overton Window paradigm shift, and how compromise is the equilibrium of political change.

Conclusion:

The following is a reflection of my personal opinions about Universal Basic Income and how they changed: I think it is important to be honest about subconscious biases. Going into this research paper, I had previously considered voting for Andrew Yang if I were ever given the chance to, that I found his UBI proposal to be a comforting progressive change, without radical and tonedeaf changes with little to no plausibility of success. My opinions about Andrew Yang have become more optimistic and I find myself more comfortable with the idea of UBI than before, though I don’t think it's obviously a “fix-all” solution. What I didn't realize is almost how genius his political marketing and strategy is, considering how history typically crowns this sort of political strategy king. I’ve learned from my historical analysis that though progress is great and we should never let up in our fight for a politically sound and cohesive world, compromises are strategically an “okay” thing to employ. Yes, it is unfortunate that we have to compromise with evil from time to time, but if you’re willing to sacrifice short term security and sometimes even safety in your fight for equality, surely short-term compromises are reasonable. I have also come to the conclusion that without something like UBI, without compromise, we will be in an endless deadlock with our adversaries and no progress will be made at all.
